## What Happened: The Pentagon Deal Controversy

On February 27, 2026, OpenAI announced a partnership with the U.S. Department of Defense to provide AI tools to the Pentagon. The deal immediately drew criticism from users, employees, and civil society organizations over concerns about potential use in domestic mass surveillance and autonomous weapons systems.

The timing amplified the backlash: the Pentagon had just dropped Anthropic as an AI contractor after Anthropic refused similar terms, citing its firm stance against using AI for mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. Anthropic's refusal was based on its position that such applications are incompatible with democratic values.

A grassroots movement called "QuitGPT" emerged in early February 2026, encouraging users to cancel subscriptions and uninstall the ChatGPT app over safety and ethical concerns. The movement gained momentum after the Pentagon deal announcement.

**The immediate impact:**
- ChatGPT uninstalls surged 295% day-over-day (vs. typical 9% daily uninstall rate)
- ChatGPT downloads dropped 13% on Saturday, then 5% on Sunday
- Claude downloads surged, reaching #1 on U.S. Apple App Store
- Nearly 900 OpenAI and Google employees signed an open letter opposing AI use in domestic mass surveillance or autonomous lethal operations without human oversight

On March 3, 2026, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman acknowledged the deal was "opportunistic and sloppy" and "rushed," admitting its "optics don't look good." OpenAI revised the contract to explicitly prohibit:
- Intentional domestic surveillance of U.S. persons and nationals
- NSA use of OpenAI systems without separate contract modification
- Use of AI models to direct autonomous weapons systems

## Why Users Are Leaving ChatGPT

The 295% surge in uninstalls reflects three converging factors:

**1. Ethical concerns about military use**
Users opposed OpenAI's Pentagon deal over fears of AI use in domestic surveillance and autonomous weapons. The QuitGPT movement framed the issue as a choice between supporting ethical AI development (Anthropic's stance) vs. enabling potential misuse (OpenAI's initial deal).

**2. Availability of competitive alternatives**
In 2024, ChatGPT had a clear quality lead. By 2026, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity offer comparable or superior capabilities in specific domains. Users no longer face a significant quality tradeoff when switching.

**3. Trust and transparency**
Sam Altman's admission that the deal was "opportunistic and sloppy" damaged user trust. The rapid contract revision suggested OpenAI prioritized the deal over user concerns, then backtracked under pressure.
